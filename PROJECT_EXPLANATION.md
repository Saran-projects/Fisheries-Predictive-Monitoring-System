## 🎣 The Problem
Overfishing and environmental changes are putting massive stress on our ocean's ecosystems. For authorities and fisheries to make sustainable decisions—like setting the right fishing quotas—they need to know how many fish are actually out there. But measuring wild fish populations across vast oceans is incredibly difficult, and waiting for manual surveys often means we react too late to population collapses.

## 💡 Our Solution
We have built an **AI-powered early warning system** for marine ecosystems. 

Our application is a smart dashboard that takes years of historical data and uses Artificial Intelligence to predict the future health of wild fish populations in specific regions (such as the Great Barrier Reef). It acts as a crystal ball for marine conservationists and fishery managers.

## ⚙️ How It Works (In Simple Terms)
Instead of guessing, our system looks at the patterns of the past to protect the future:

1. **The Brain (AI Model):** We feed historical data—including the year, region, and type of fishery—into a machine learning model. The AI learns the complex trends and patterns of how fish populations grow and shrink over time.
2. **The Prediction:** A user can select a specific region and year, and the AI will estimate the total weight of the wild fish population (the "biomass") for that specific scenario.
3. **The Risk Assessor:** The system automatically compares this prediction against historical averages. It then gives an easy-to-understand health grade:
   - 🟢 **Sustainable:** Fish populations are healthy.
   - 🟠 **Warning:** Populations are dropping. Action is recommended (e.g., reduce fishing quotas by 30%).
   - 🔴 **Critical:** Populations are dangerously low. Immediate action is needed (e.g., halt fishing entirely).
4. **The Visual Dashboard:** Everything is presented on an interactive geographic map, making it easy for decision-makers to see exactly which zones are at risk and need immediate protection.

## 🌍 Why It Matters
This project takes the guesswork out of ocean conservation. By transforming raw, complicated data into clear, actionable insights, we can prevent overfishing *before* it happens. It helps ensure that we protect marine biodiversity while sustaining the fishing communities that rely on healthy oceans for their livelihoods.

**In one sentence:** *It's a smart map that uses AI to predict where fish populations are in danger, so we can step in and protect them before it's too late.*
